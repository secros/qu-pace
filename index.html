FAQ

What is running pace?

Running pace is how long it takes you to cover one unit of distance — typically expressed as minutes per mile (min/mi) or minutes per kilometer (min/km). For example, a 9:00/mi pace means you run each mile in 9 minutes. Pace is the inverse of speed: a faster runner has a lower (smaller) pace number.

How do I calculate my pace from a race finish time?

Divide your total finish time (in seconds) by the distance (in miles or km). Convert the result back to minutes and seconds. For example, finishing a 5K (3.107 miles) in 30:00 (1800 seconds): 1800 ÷ 3.107 = 579.5 sec/mile = 9:39/mile pace. Our calculator does this instantly.

What is a good running pace for beginners?

Most beginners run at 10–14 minutes per mile (6:15–8:42 per km). A comfortable conversational pace — where you can speak in full sentences — is ideal for building base fitness. As you progress, you'll naturally speed up. For a 5K, finishing under 40 minutes (about 12:50/mi) is a great beginner goal.

What pace do I need to qualify for the Boston Marathon?

Boston qualifying standards vary by age and sex. For men 18–34, the standard is 3:00:00 (about 6:52/mile). For women 18–34, it's 3:30:00 (about 8:01/mile). Standards get more lenient with age. Note that you typically need to beat the qualifier by 2–5+ minutes to actually receive a bib due to demand.

What is a negative split in running?

A negative split is when you run the second half of a race faster than the first half. It's widely considered the optimal race strategy because it prevents early burnout and allows you to finish strong. Most elite runners and coaches recommend starting slightly slower than goal pace and gradually increasing effort through the race.

How do I convert pace between miles and kilometers?

To convert min/mile to min/km, divide by 1.60934. To convert min/km to min/mile, multiply by 1.60934. For example, a 9:00/mile pace = 9:00 ÷ 1.60934 = 5:35/km. Our calculator handles this conversion automatically — just select your preferred unit.
